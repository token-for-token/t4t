01

FIRST PRINCIPLES

The protocol is honest. The interface should be too. No gradient marketing, no hero illustrations of glowing brains, no "AI for humanity."

001
Show the wire.

Hashes, addresses, gas, latency, escrow balances — surface them. The user is technical. Hiding the machinery insults them.

002
No decoration.

Every line, badge, and color must encode signal. Hairlines separate domains. Orange means value moving. Green means settled.

003
Grid, not page.

The layout reads as a terminal log, a contract, or an order book — not a marketing site. Dense, monospaced, scrollable.

004
Pay attention to the dot.

Small status indicators — pulses, blink-cursors, single-pixel separators — carry the mood. Brutalism is about restraint, not noise.

005
Italic is the human.

Mono is the machine voice. Newsreader italic is the editorial human voice — used only for prose. The contrast is the system.

006
Black is a color.

The background is #0a0a0a — not pure black. Pure black is flat. T4T's black has weight. So does the type.

07

RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

The aesthetic dies fast if it drifts. These are the guardrails.

DO

  • Use orange only for value moving (CTAs, xBZZ, payment).
  • Show real hashes, truncated middle: 0xa9f3…2b1c.
  • Lead with mono. Switch to italic Newsreader for prose only.
  • Keep all corners sharp. border-radius: 0.
  • Use 1px hairlines to separate domains, not boxes-in-boxes.
  • Pulse the live dot. It's the only ambient animation.

DON'T

  • No purple gradients. No glassmorphism. No blob shapes.
  • No stock illustrations of robots, brains, or networks.
  • Never use Inter, Roboto, or any humanist sans.
  • No drop shadows. Depth comes from hairlines and layering.
  • Don't soften technical truths. Show gas, stake, slashing.
  • Avoid more than three accent colors on one screen.
